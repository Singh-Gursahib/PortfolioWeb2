Verbi — AI-Powered Study Companion

Verbi transforms passive study materials — lecture notes, textbooks, PDFs — into active learning experiences. Using LLM-powered document parsing, it generates adaptive quizzes that adjust to each student's proficiency level in real time.

// Overview

The problem & solution

University students often struggle with passive study habits — rereading notes without actively engaging with the material. Research consistently shows that active recall (testing yourself) is far more effective than passive review, but creating practice questions is tedious and time-consuming.

Verbi automates this entirely. Students upload their course materials — PDFs, slide decks, typed notes — and the AI parses the content, identifies key concepts, and generates a pool of adaptive quiz questions tailored to the specific subject matter.

As the student answers questions, Verbi's difficulty engine adjusts in real time. Concepts the student has mastered appear less frequently, while weak areas are reinforced with additional questions from different angles. The result: more efficient study sessions and better retention.

// Architecture

How it's built

Verbi follows a modern serverless architecture with a React/Next.js frontend, Firebase for auth and real-time database, and Google Gemini for the AI backbone. The system processes documents through a multi-step pipeline:

  • Document Ingestion: PDF.js parses uploaded documents into structured text chunks, preserving headings and hierarchy.
  • Concept Extraction: Gemini identifies key concepts, creates a topic graph, and assigns difficulty weights based on complexity.
  • Question Generation: For each concept, the LLM generates multiple question types with distractors for MCQs and rubrics for open-ended answers.
  • Adaptive Engine: A spaced-repetition scheduler (modified SM-2 algorithm) determines which questions to surface next based on past performance.
  • Feedback Loop: Student responses are fed back to refine difficulty estimates and generate targeted follow-up questions for weak areas.
